Yoga: Our Ancient Heritage

Yoga is one of the six orthodox philosophical schools of Hinduism, originating in ancient India over 5,000 years ago. Derived from the Sanskrit root "Yuj", yoga means union ‚Äì the union of the individual self with the universal consciousness.

The first references to yoga appear in the Rig Veda. Later, the Upanishads and the Bhagavad Gita emphasized the spiritual and meditative aspects. Patanjali systematized yoga practices through his Yoga Sutras in 200 BCE, introducing the 8 limbs of Ashtanga Yoga.

Over the centuries, yoga evolved through classical, post-classical, and modern phases, adapting to contemporary lifestyles while preserving its traditional essence.
